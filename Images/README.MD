Cyclistic Bike-Share: User Conversion Strategy
Project Overview
This case study analyzes historical trip data from the Cyclistic bike-share program to design marketing strategies aimed at converting casual riders (Customers) into annual members (Subscribers).

1. Demographic Analysis (The "Who")
Title: User Profile Analysis: Demographic Breakdown by Gender and Membership

I analyzed gender distribution to understand the makeup of our two user groups.

While Subscribers skew toward a specific demographic, casual Customers show a more balanced distribution, representing a major opportunity for inclusive marketing.

2. Geographic Analysis (The "Where")
Title: Top 10 High-Traffic Stations: Key Conversion Hubs for Casual Riders

I identified the top starting stations to pinpoint where casual riders are most active.

These "Conversion Hubs" are the primary locations recommended for physical marketing deployment.

3. Temporal Analysis (The "When")
Title: The Golden Window Discovery: Peak Hour Trends

Using a custom SUBSTR formula in Looker Studio, I extracted trip start hours to find usage patterns.

Subscribers: Peak during commute hours (07-08 AM and 16-17 PM).

Customers: Peak during the "Golden Window" (11 AM – 17 PM).

Final Recommendation
To maximize conversion of the 22,000 "Unknown" Customers, Cyclistic should deploy marketing street teams to the Top 10 Stations specifically during the 11 AM – 3 PM midday peak.
